Hagia Sophia

A former patriarchal basilica, later a mosque, and now a museum in Istanbul, Türkiye. Famous for its massive dome, it's a prime example of Byzantine architecture.

Originally built as a Christian cathedral in the 6th century AD, Hagia Sophia served as the seat of the Eastern Orthodox Church for nearly a millennium before being converted into a mosque in the 15th century following the Ottoman conquest of Constantinople. Its conversion to a museum in 1935 under the secular Republic of Türkiye made it accessible to all, showcasing its rich history and architectural grandeur. The building's design, particularly its innovative use of pendentives to support the dome, has influenced architectural styles worldwide for centuries. Inside, visitors can marvel at the intricate mosaics, massive columns, and the sheer scale of the interior space, which reflects the power and artistry of the Byzantine and Ottoman empires. Hagia Sophia remains a symbol of Istanbul's complex history and cultural diversity, attracting millions of visitors each year.
